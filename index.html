SurgAM: Surgical Affordance Map Prediction with Multimodal Feature Fusion for Robot Autonomy

Lei Song1, Yonghao Long1, Mengya Xu1, Jiayi Geng2, Xiuyuan Chen2†, Qi Dou1†
1The Chinese University of Hong Kong, 2Peking University People's Hospital
†Corresponding authors
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

SurgAM predicts surgical affordance maps to enable autonomous robot manipulation in surgical environments.

Abstract

Affordance perception is a foundational capability for intelligent surgical robots, enabling the identification of actionable regions in surgical scenes based on task-specific requirements. Recent advances have introduced dense affordance representations that provide fine-grained visual guidance, but most existing methods rely solely on RGB input, overlooking complementary modalities such as depth and tool segmentation that can enhance scene understanding.

To address these limitations, we propose SurgAM, a multimodal surgical affordance prediction framework that explicitly integrates RGB, depth, and tool mask features for dense affordance estimation. Our method leverages DINOv2 as the visual backbone and incorporates a depth encoding module and tool mask prompts to enrich the feature representation, followed by a lightweight MLP-based decoder for pixel-wise affordance regression.

Extensive experiments on a challenging surgical affordance dataset demonstrate that SurgAM outperforms state-of-the-art methods in all metrics and generalizes well to unseen surgical actions. Furthermore, we validate the effectiveness of SurgAM through real-world experiments on the da Vinci Research Kit (dVRK), achieving autonomous grasping and retraction tasks guided solely by predicted affordance maps.
